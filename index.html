GoHighLevel — marketing engine
Owns: leads, nurture, comms, routing, reputation
Every customer enquiry enters here. GHL qualifies it and routes through one of three pathways — Urgent fires immediately to SM8, Core goes direct, VIP books a consultation first. GHL also owns post-job review requests and unconverted quote follow-up sequences. It does not touch operations, invoicing, or reporting.
Current gap: The GHL → SM8 Make automation is partially built. Manual intervention is filling the gap between lead qualification and job card creation. This is the first and most critical fix.
ServiceM8 — operational core
Owns: jobs, scheduling, QM, quotes, invoices, time logs
Every job card lives here from creation to completion. Partners receive jobs, complete QM checklists, raise quotes, invoice onsite, and log time. Scheduling — diary management, capacity rules, engineer allocation — should be fully controlled from SM8 by the office, not by engineers in the field.
Current gap: Scheduling rules and job-type blocking are not configured. Engineers are self-scheduling. This creates white space, uneven utilisation, and no office visibility of diary capacity.
Burdi — materials & supplier engine
Owns: POs, supplier invoices, partner materials accounts, cost allocation
Every material purchase flows through Burdi. Partners raise POs using their Partner Order Code plus SM8 job number. Burdi matches the supplier invoice to the PO, allocates cost to the correct job and partner, then pushes the bill to Xero and costs to Wink. The job-linking rule is what makes financial reporting accurate.
Current gap: Missing job numbers on POs break the Burdi→Xero→Wink cost flow entirely. The Red Flag Report must be live and actioned daily. Without it, margin leakage is invisible.
Xero — compliance only
Owns: VAT, CIS, payroll, bank reconciliation, statutory accounts
Xero receives from SM8 (invoices), Burdi (bills), Dext (expenses), and Monzo (bank). It processes CIS, handles VAT, and produces statutory accounts. It does not produce business intelligence — that is Wink's job. Keeping Xero as a compliance-only ledger is architecturally correct and must remain so.
Wink — single reporting truth
Owns: all KPIs, dashboards, partner P&L, job profitability, attribution
Wink is the CEO dashboard. It pulls from every system and produces sales KPIs, partner leaderboards, financial P&L, category profitability, marketing ROI, and the Red Flag Report. When fully live, Mark can see the profit on every job, which partners are performing, and which lead sources convert — in real time, without a spreadsheet.
Current gap: Wink is showing no data in the audit view. The data pipelines from all four upstream systems are not yet flowing correctly. The reporting layer exists but the pipes have not been built. This is the sprint's core deliverable.

High severity · Direct revenue loss

High severity · Direct revenue loss

Invoice and payment
ServiceM8 → Xero
Partner invoices onsite including labour, materials, and MJP margins. Takes payment through SM8. Invoice auto-syncs to Xero overnight.
Gap: If partners are not invoicing through SM8 consistently, the Xero sync breaks and payment data in Wink is unreliable.
6
Partner earnings calculated
Wink
Wink calculates full partner P&L: points earnings (£20–£22/point by tier), 10% quote commission, 10% materials margin share. Full monthly P&L with CIS and net payout.
Gap: Earnings accuracy depends on SM8, Burdi, and Xero all being clean. Any upstream gap creates discrepancies that erode partner trust.

Sprint plan
Four phases. Six weeks. One working system.
Fixes are sequenced by revenue impact and dependency. Nothing in Phase 2 gets built until Phase 1 is stable. Wink comes last because it is only as good as the data flowing into it.

Merge duplicate contacts and establish entry rules to prevent recurrence
Implement call tracking numbers per source — website, billboard, referral, Google profile
